Harbin Ice and Snow Festival

The World's Largest Ice Festival

About the Festival

The Harbin Ice and Snow Festival is held every winter in Harbin, China. It is one of the largest winter festivals in the world and features massive ice and snow sculptures.

The festival officially begins in early January and lasts until late February. Temperatures often drop below -20°C, which helps preserve the ice structures.

Ice Sculptures

Artists cut huge blocks of ice from the Songhua River and carve them into castles, monuments, and famous landmarks.

At night, colorful lights are placed inside the sculptures, turning the entire park into a glowing ice city.

Activities

Visitors can enjoy ice slides, skating, snow sports, and cultural performances. There are also snow sculpture exhibitions and winter swimming demonstrations.

Plan Your Visit

Location: Harbin, Heilongjiang Province, China

Best time to visit: January

Wear heavy winter clothing due to extreme cold temperatures.
